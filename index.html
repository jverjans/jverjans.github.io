About

I am a clinician-scientist at the Royal Adelaide Hospital whose work sits at the intersection of medicine, imaging and machine learning. I combine training in molecular medicine and clinical cardiology with years of collaboration alongside engineers and computer scientists, with one goal: translating advanced AI research into real clinical impact.

I graduated in medicine from Maastricht University via an MD–PhD track after a DiPalma Fellowship with Professor Jagat Narula in Philadelphia and UC Irvine. During cardiology training I was awarded a Rubicon Fellowship by the Dutch Science Foundation for a post-doctoral fellowship at Massachusetts General Hospital / Harvard Medical School, followed by a clinician-scientist award at University Medical Centre Utrecht. I was recruited by the University of Adelaide in 2017 and received the University's Future Industry Making Fellowship in 2022.

I founded and lead the Medical Machine Learning group at the Australian Institute for Machine Learning (AIML) — now 40+ clinicians, biomedical researchers and computer scientists — with the aim of making the University of Adelaide a global leader in AI for healthcare. The group has won international technology challenges and holds research contracts with companies including GSK, Roche, Siemens Healthineers and Medtronic.

Research & Projects

My group applies machine learning to medical imaging and clinical data — from anomaly detection and image synthesis to interpretable, trustworthy AI — and runs the translational projects that bring these tools into hospital workflows across South Australia.

RapidX AI

The world's first randomised controlled trial of AI-supported assessment of suspected cardiac chest pain in the emergency department.

Automedic

SA Health's largest AI project — automating the review of patients across the South Australian health system.

Cardiomics

Improving guideline adherence and outcomes after myocardial infarction through data-driven cardiology.

Medical VQA Challenge

Our team won the NIH global Medical Visual Question Answering challenge — an early forerunner of today's medical multimodal AI.
